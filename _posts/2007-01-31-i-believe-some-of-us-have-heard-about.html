--- layout: post title: Metisse Desktop date: '2007-01-31T21:54:00.000+08:00' author: izhar firdaus tags: - Linux - Open Source modified_time: '2007-02-01T08:55:52.905+08:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-28371165.post-5079685164334027554 blogger_orig_url: https://blog.kagesenshi.org/2007/01/i-believe-some-of-us-have-heard-about.html --- I believe some of us have heard about Metisse desktop environment thats going to be provided by Mandriva to their users. Metisse is quite an old project (since FC1) but havent had any major release until recently. Metisse is designed with usability in mind rather than funky effects - a product of the team's research on Human Computer Interaction subject. It won't make shiny effects but it might help you (and grandmas) to use computer and the desktop easier. See screenshots here

If you're interested with it, theres still no Metisse 0.4 packages for distros other that Mandriva thats has been out in the public yet. However, you can build it from source by following this Howto.

Sadly, It uses a modified Xserver (rather than available compositing from AIGLX/XGL) which immediately turned me off. I don't like polluting my system with from-source installations that might be hard to remove later.
